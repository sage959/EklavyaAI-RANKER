The Problem

Resumes are claims.
Proof of work is evidence.

Most ATS tools match keywords. Eklavya AI retrieves, evaluates, and explains fit based on what candidates have actually done.

The Old Way

Keyword-based filtering misses the point

  • ATS filters by keyword presence, not capability depth
  • Strong candidates who write plainly get silently dropped
  • No explanation for why a candidate was shortlisted or rejected
  • Rankings are opaque — recruiters must still read every resume
vs
Eklavya AI

Evidence-backed ranking you can trust

  • Retrieves specific evidence of skills from candidate portfolios and history
  • Hybrid BM25 + semantic retrieval finds substance, not just phrasing
  • Every candidate gets a grounded reason — not a black-box score
  • Recruiters spend time deciding, not reading

How It Works

Three steps from
JD to shortlist.

Paste a job description. Eklavya AI retrieves, scores, and explains — in seconds.

01

Parse the JD

The system extracts role requirements, skill signals, and contextual intent from your job description — beyond surface keywords.

02

Retrieve candidate evidence

Hybrid BM25 + semantic search pulls specific evidence from candidate-centric knowledge chunks — projects, outputs, and demonstrated work.

03

Rank with explainable AI

A cross-encoder reranker scores each candidate against the JD. The output is a ranked shortlist with grounded, human-readable reasoning per candidate.

Why Trust It

Every score has a story.
Every shortlist is grounded.

Eklavya AI doesn't hand you a number. It shows you the evidence that produced it.

Proof-of-work scoring

Scores are built from specific candidate outputs — shipped projects, published work, and demonstrated skills — not just keyword frequency on a resume.

Candidate-centric RAG

Knowledge is chunked and indexed per candidate. Retrieval surfaces the most relevant evidence for each specific JD, not a generic profile summary.

Hybrid retrieval + reranking

BM25 captures exact term matches. Semantic search captures meaning and context. A cross-encoder reranker fuses both signals for the final score. No single method dominates; the best evidence wins.

Semantic similarity 91%

Behavioral signals

Recruiter responsiveness, prior outreach history, and engagement patterns surface candidates who are not only capable but genuinely reachable and motivated.

Grounded evidence

Every reasoning snippet is traced back to a specific chunk of candidate data. Recruiters can read the source, not just the conclusion.
